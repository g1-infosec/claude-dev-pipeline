# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We want participation in this project to be a harassment-free experience for
everyone, regardless of experience level, background, identity, or how they
choose to build. Contributors and maintainers commit to keeping the project a
respectful, constructive space.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful and assume good faith.
- Give and accept feedback on the work, not the person.
- Prefer clarity over cleverness when explaining or reviewing.
- Respect that people have different levels of experience; this project is aimed
  in part at people who build by prompting, and "I don't know how X works" is a
  welcome thing to say.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks, public or private.
- Discriminatory, demeaning, or sexualised language or imagery.
- Publishing others' private information without permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or reviews.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions) and when
an individual is representing the project in public.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report unacceptable behaviour to **conduct@g1-infosec.example** (replace with the
real address before publishing). Reports are handled confidentially.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards and may remove, edit, or
reject contributions, comments, or contributors that violate this code, and may
temporarily or permanently ban anyone for behaviour they judge harmful.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is intentionally short and written for this project. If you
would rather adopt an ecosystem-standard document, the Contributor Covenant
(https://www.contributor-covenant.org) is a common drop-in replacement.
